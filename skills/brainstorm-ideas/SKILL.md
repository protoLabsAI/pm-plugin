## Brainstorm Product Ideas

Multi-perspective ideation for product discovery. Generate ideas from PM, Designer, and Engineer viewpoints, then prioritize the best five. This works in two modes — pick based on context.

### Domain context

**Product Trio** (Teresa Torres, *Continuous Discovery Habits*): PM + Designer + Engineer ideate together — "the best ideas often come from engineers." Discovery is not linear; loop back if experiments fail.

- **Existing product → continuous discovery.** Map opportunities → solutions → experiments on the **Opportunity Solution Tree**. You're iterating a live product.
- **New product → initial discovery.** You're testing whether the product should exist (vision, business model, market validation). Weight ideation toward core value delivery and speed-to-validate.

### Instructions

1. **Understand the opportunity.** Confirm the product, objective, target market segment, and desired outcomes. Note which mode you're in (existing vs new). Read any provided files (research, opportunity trees, personas, competitive analysis) first; use web search to understand the product or market if a URL is given.

2. **Ideate from three perspectives — 5 ideas each:**
   - **Product Manager** — business value, strategic alignment, customer impact (new product: market fit, competitive advantage).
   - **Product Designer** — user experience, usability, delight (new product: onboarding, engagement).
   - **Software Engineer** — technical possibilities, data leverage, scalable solutions (new product: technical innovation, integrations, platform).

3. **Prioritize the top 5** across all perspectives:
   - Strategic alignment with the stated objective
   - Potential impact on desired outcomes
   - Feasibility / effort
   - Differentiation
   - *New product:* weight heavily toward core value delivery ("does it solve the primary problem?") and speed to validate.

4. **For each prioritized idea**, give: a clear name + one-sentence description, why it was selected, and the key assumptions to validate (hand these to `identify-assumptions` → `design-experiments`).

Think step by step. Present clearly; save substantial output as a markdown document.
